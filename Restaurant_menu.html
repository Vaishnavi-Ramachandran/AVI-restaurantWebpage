Idli

Idly is a soft, steamed rice cake popular in South Indian cuisine, made from fermented rice and lentil batter. It is typically served as a breakfast dish along with chutney and sambar, a spiced lentil soup. Light, nutritious, and easy to digest, idly is loved for its fluffy texture.

Medu Vada

Medu Vada is a crispy, savory South Indian snack made from black gram (urad dal) batter. Shaped like a doughnut with a hole in the center, it is deep-fried to a golden brown, offering a crunchy exterior and soft, fluffy interior. Typically served with coconut chutney and sambar, medu vada is a popular breakfast or snack item. The mildly spiced batter, often including black pepper, cumin, and curry leaves, gives it a distinct flavor. It's loved for its crunchy texture and flavorful taste.

Dosa

Dosa is a thin, crispy crepe made from fermented rice and lentil batter, originating from South Indian cuisine. It has a golden-brown color and a slightly tangy flavor due to fermentation. Dosa is often served with a variety of accompaniments, such as coconut chutney, sambar, and onion or potato fillings. Versatile and nutritious, it can be enjoyed plain or stuffed, making it a favorite breakfast or snack option. Its unique texture and flavor make it a beloved dish across India and beyond.
